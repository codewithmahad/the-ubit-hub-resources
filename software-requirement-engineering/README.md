# SE453: Software Requirement Engineering (SRE)

This directory contains comprehensive resources, course materials, and reference books for the Software Requirement Engineering (SE453) course. The repository is structured to provide quick access to specific study materials, ranging from official textbooks to student-curated handwritten notes.

## Directory Structure

### Root Directory Files
- **SE453_SRE_Course_Outline.pdf**: The official course outline detailing the syllabus, grading scheme, and topics covered throughout the semester.
- **SE453_SRE_Comprehensive_Notes_ShaikhMahad.pdf**: A comprehensive typed collection of notes covering the course material.
- **SRE Notes.pdf**: Additional general course notes for quick reference.

### `handwritten-notes/`
Contains scanned copies of physical notes taken during lectures.
- **SE453_SRE_Handwritten_Notes_ShaikhMahad_Complete.pdf**: Complete handwritten notes covering the entire semester's curriculum.

### `reference-books/`
Standard textbooks recommended for the course.
- **Software_Engineering_Pressman_7th_Edition.pdf**: "Software Engineering: A Practitioner's Approach" by Roger S. Pressman (7th Edition).
- **Software_Engineering_Sommerville_10th_Edition.pdf**: "Software Engineering" by Ian Sommerville (10th Edition).
- **Springer_Requirements_Engineering_2nd_Edition.pdf**: "Requirements Engineering" (2nd Edition) published by Springer.

#### `reference-books/extracted-chapters/`
Specific chapters extracted from the main textbooks for focused reading and quick revision.
- **Pressman_7th_Ed_Chapter_5.pdf**: Understanding Requirements
- **Pressman_7th_Ed_Chapter_6.pdf**: Requirements Modeling: Scenarios, Information, and Analysis Classes
- **Pressman_7th_Ed_Chapter_7.pdf**: Requirements Modeling: Flow, Behavior, Patterns, and WebApps
- **Sommerville_10th_Ed_Chapter_4_Requirement_Engineering.pdf**: Requirements Engineering Processes and Elicitation

## Study Guide & Recommendations

- Start by reviewing the **SE453_SRE_Course_Outline.pdf** to understand the scope and expectations of the course.
- Use the files in **extracted-chapters** for targeted reading on core SRE topics without needing to navigate the heavy full-textbook PDFs.
- Cross-reference the **handwritten notes** with the **comprehensive typed notes** to solidify your understanding of lecture-specific discussions.
